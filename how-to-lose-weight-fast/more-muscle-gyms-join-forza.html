More muscle gyms join Forza

Posted on November 15, 2011 by N Giannini There have been 0 comments

Forza, the Number #1 T5 selling weight loss brand is proud to announce its affiliation with Pro-Fitness gym in Chesterfield. The gym is owned by Andy James, United Kingdom Winner , Class 2 overall Britain 2008, Mr Universe class 2 2008 2nd place, Central Britain class 2 and overall.

In a recent article The Big Gyms Join Forza we explained how major muscle gyms were loving the Forza T5 brand and enjoying the benefits associated with T5. Pro Fitness Chesterfield is the latest in a long line of gyms joining the trend.

Forza has expanded its network of resellers rapidly over the past 3 months and it shows no sign of slowing down Steve Atkins, New Business Manager for Forza say's "As a salesperson it always makes my job easier when I can sell something that works and that I can believe in. I have experienced the benefits of T5 myself and sell the products in my hair salon in Derby. Once I saw the benefits of the product I began to work with Forza and now I spend most of my week signing up new resellers. The product literally flies off the shelves and I’m excited about the New Year because it is a very busy time".

Forza has experienced growth in trade sales to both hair salons and traditional muscle gyms in the past 3 months. The T5 range is very popular and Forza aims to expand its range to include protein and creatine based products. The market for food supplements is still growing as people look to become healthier as an escape from the gloom of economic crisis.

"We have definitely seen uplift in people joining the gym and taking an interest in their personal fitness here at Pro-Fitness Chesterfield. We sell the Forza T5 Blacks in the gym and the regulars are loving them. Our gym is what you might call a 'serious gym' for  customers who want to achieve the highest standards and know that they are being supported by a strong team of trainers, so it makes sense for us to sell the best T5's on the market today", Andy James, Proprietor, Pro Fitness, Chesterfield.

The food supplement business, which produces vitamin pills and herbal supplements, is worth $50bn (£13.5bn) worldwide. Research published by the Food Standards Agency last year found that a third of people in the UK were taking vitamin and mineral supplements. Americans spend around $6bn (£4bn) a year on vitamins.
